# 5 Freunde — Canonical Lore

> A living compendium of the world, characters, factions, artifacts, and story arcs of *5 Freunde*. Sources: `MemoryStories.js`, `Museum.js`, `EvilHeroes.js`, `AltarData.js`, the Story scripts under `dlc/*/Story.js`, and the DLC briefs under `tasks/`.

---

## 1. The World

The world of 5 Freunde is a layered reality where **elemental biomes** are not just geography but psychological truths. Each landscape — volcanic wastes, deep oceans, frozen plateaus, living jungles, industrial graveyards — is a mirror of a state of mind. Five primary elements run through everything: **Fire, Water, Ice, Plant, Metal**. They are at once forces of nature and the inner languages of the people destiny chooses.

Behind the visible world lies the **Shadow Realm**, a parallel existence governed not by evil but by *honesty*. Fear lives there because fear is real. The Shadow Realm was never meant to be a prison; it was meant to be a counterweight. The boundary between realms used to be tended by keepers, the most famous (and infamous) of whom was **Makuta**.

Two artifacts pin the cosmology in place:

- **The Golden Mask** — forged as a symbol of unity between the Light and Shadow Realms, but in practice it became a tool of suppression: it allowed Light to dominate Shadow without understanding it. Multiple factions are now hunting it, each convinced it means something different.
- **The Chaos Gem** — an unstable relic of the old world, said to have been destroyed. It pulses with infinite potential energy. Touching it grants visions; gathering it grants reality-rewriting power. It is the wedge by which the Entropy Mage threatens to undo existence.

## 2. The Five Friends

Five ordinary people, in a modern city, were each chosen by an element on the worst night of their lives. They didn't ask for it. They became friends before they became heroes, and they stayed friends after.

### Fire — The Impulsive One
Son of a firefighter. Always felt too much, reacted too fast. The night a high-rise burned out of control, he ran in without thinking, and the fire bent away from his skin as if it recognized him. His trial took place in a volcanic wasteland that taught him the hardest lesson fire can teach: **destruction is easy; creation requires restraint.** He came back steadier, purposeful.

### Water — The Listener
The girl who always heard everyone before she spoke. Quiet glue of the group. Caught in a flood while helping evacuate, dragged under by a current that should have killed her — but the water *slowed*, as if it had decided to spare her. Her trial came beneath the ocean where light barely reaches; she learned that **depth is not weakness, but resilience**, and that fear can pass through you without taking control.

### Ice — The Logical One
Always tried to understand things before acting; logic felt safer than emotion. Cold fascinated him because it follows strict rules. His power awoke in a snowstorm blackout that should have killed him — instead the cold wrapped around him like armor. His trial in a frozen wasteland nearly froze him spiritually as well as physically: he learned that **control without connection leads only to stillness**, and forced himself to remember laughter and warmth.

### Plant — The Watcher
The quiet one who preferred to see how things unfolded naturally. Volunteering at a community garden when a sinkhole opened, he reached for the ground without thinking, and roots erupted to stop the collapse. His trial in a cutthroat jungle taught him that **stewardship is not the same as control** — sometimes growth must be guided, and sometimes it must be cut back.

### Metal — The Protector
The one who jokes first and thinks later, because silence makes fear too loud. Flirts with Water shamelessly. Trapped in a collapsing construction site, his body hardened — metal fused to his skin like it had always belonged. He laughed hysterically afterward; laughing felt better than shaking. His trial in an industrial graveyard taught him that **metal that bends survives longer than metal that resists everything**. Destiny doesn't always choose heroes; sometimes it chooses shields.

The Five fit together because their flaws fit together. Fire burns too hot, Ice thinks too much, Plant feels too deeply, Water holds them together, and Metal hides his fear behind jokes.

## 3. The Black Hero

A sixth, unaffiliated figure walks between the worlds. He doesn't remember the moment he became something else — only the moment he understood he could never go back. The night the city lost power, the shadows moved with intention. He stepped closer; the world folded inward. He crossed into the Shadow Realm, where fear is *substance* rather than emotion.

Something vast watched him there. **Makuta did not introduce himself. He did not need to.**

The Black Hero now wanders between worlds, drawn toward the Golden Mask — which radiates something the shadows cannot consume, and which therefore unsettles them. Each step deeper into the Shadow Realm erases more of his past: faces blur, names lose meaning. He fears forgetting *why any of it matters*.

## 4. The Antagonists

### Makuta — The Fallen Keeper

Before he was Makuta, he was a keeper of balance, older than heroes, tasked with guarding the boundary between Light and Shadow. **The Shadow Realm was never meant to be evil. It was meant to be honest.**

He watched mortal civilizations rise by *denying* their own darkness — burying fear, guilt, and violence under laws and stories. Buried things rot. He saw children inherit fear they never created. He saw heroes praised for violence if it wore the right color. When he tried to warn the guardians of Light, they dismissed fear as weakness. When the realms began bleeding into each other, they blamed the Shadow Realm — never themselves.

The forging of the **Golden Mask** was the moment everything broke. It was sold as a bridge between realms; in practice it silenced fear instead of addressing it. Makuta refused to protect it and was branded a traitor. Exile followed.

Alone in the Shadow Realm, he listened to what fear truly was: *memory, instinct, the voice that says something is wrong.* The realm did not corrupt him — **it educated him.** He concluded that balance could no longer be restored gently. The Mask must be reclaimed and used to merge the Shadow Realm with the physical world — *not to end life, but to end denial*. A world that sees its fear cannot pretend innocence.

He has begun gathering those rejected by balance.

### Green Goblin — The Grieving Genius

Always the smartest person in any room, and rooms never forgave him for it. Built his first explosive at fourteen just to see if he could. Believed technology was the only honest language, because machines do exactly what you design them to do. **People disappoint. Machines don't.**

His son — sharper than him, gentler than him — was the one exception. The day the biome conflict reached their city, the Goblin was in his workshop. His son was not. *They never found enough of him to bury.*

Six weeks after the funeral, Makuta found him building something he intended to use on a city. Makuta offered purpose; he accepted, because *grief without direction is just poison.* But the Goblin is double-dealing. He has spent three years studying the Mask's resonance frequency while Makuta studied its mythology. **Makuta wants worship. The Goblin wants compliance.** He intends to take the Mask for himself — and when he does, the first thing he will do is laugh, not at the world, but at himself, for waiting so long.

### The Entropy Mage

A primordial unmaker who wishes to reduce reality to *total zero*. Glimpsed by Gravity and Void in a vision granted by the Chaos Gem; defeated by them at the climax of *Champions of Chaos*. Reality itself crumbles in his presence; only an unlikely partnership can keep it together.

## 5. The Museum — Hall of Memories

The Museum is the meta-narrative of the game: a sanctuary built for *remembering*. It is divided by hero (Fire, Water, Ice, Plant, Metal), with a central **Gallery** for DLC heroes, a **Jail** wing displaying captured creatures as specimens, and a **Forbidden Basement** that houses the memory shards of Makuta and the Green Goblin behind locks and switches — the world fears what they represent.

Each hero's wing holds dozens of **Memory Shards**: short first-person monologues that reveal fears, doubts, growth moments, and the moments destiny chose them. Memory recovery is the meta-progression: discovering not what each hero *can do*, but what made each hero *who they are*.

Heroes wandering each other's rooms react in character — Fire complains about the damp in Water's room; Water finds Fire's room unbearable; Ice notes the bleak beauty of frost-killed plants; Metal admires the precision of his own joints and seams. The Museum is, in the end, **a metaphor for understanding oneself through artifacts and reflection.**

## 6. The Altar of Mastery

Each hero has an Altar with a personal upgrade tree: cooldown reductions, then radius/duration boosts, then a transformative final upgrade (Lingering Flame, Tsunami, Shatter, Thornmail, Magnetic Field). Beyond the personal trees lie the **Convergence Mutations** — synergies unlocked by mastering combinations of elements. Examples:

- **Boiling Wave** (Fire + Water) — Tidal Wave applies Fire DoT
- **Frostbite Armor** (Ice + Metal) — Iron Will freezes attackers
- **Wildfire** (Fire + Plant) — Overgrowth applies Burn
- **Permafrost** (Water + Ice) — Deep Freeze pushes enemies
- **Hydro-Shield** (Water + Metal) — Tidal Wave grants temporary HP
- **Ironbark** (Plant + Metal) — Overgrowth grants damage reduction
- (and more across all ten pairings)

Mechanically the Altar is upgrades; thematically it is **the journey from isolated strength to harmonized teamwork.**

## 7. The DLC Sagas

The DLCs expand the world outward. Some are character packs that expand the roster; others are full 50-wave story campaigns that add entire factions, biomes, villains, and philosophies.

### Rise of the Rock — Earth Hero
Momentum-based knight. A roster expansion; no story campaign.

### Tournament of Thunder — Lightning Hero
Speed and chain-lightning. A roster expansion; the campaign is a tournament structure rather than a narrative arc.

### Champions of Chaos — *The Artifact* (Gravity & Void)

Two rivals turned brothers-in-arms.

- **Arc 1 — The Artifact:** Gravity senses an ancient pull; Void detects an energy signature that doesn't belong to this reality. Together they uncover the **Chaos Gem**, long thought destroyed.
- **Arc 2 — The Encounter:** They clash over it. Gravity warns Void back; Void calls him foolish. Enemies swarm and force a truce.
- **Arc 3 — The Revelation:** The Gem shows them a vision of **the Entropy Mage** rewriting reality. Probability of success alone, Void calculates: 0%. As a unit: 14%. *Acceptable.*
- **Arc 4 — The Bond:** They become genuine companions. Void takes a hit meant for Gravity; Gravity protects Void's core while it reboots. Void: *"The term 'friend' was previously undefined. Updating dictionary."*
- **Arc 5 — Entropy Falls:** They breach the throne room and stop the Mage.

**Theme:** *Those who fight alone for balance can achieve it together.*

### Faith of Fortune — *Two Sides of a Coin* (Spirit & Chance)

A monk and a gambler racing to the True Golden Mask.

- **Arc 1 — The Split Path:** Spirit pursues the thief who breached the sacred seals; Chance tears open a rift to the Fields of Madness, where logic dissolves and gravity shifts. They spot each other across a chasm. *A silent vow is made: one will stop the other.*
- **Arc 2 — The Alliance & The Hunt:** Chance hires the **Green Goblin** to slow Spirit down. Spirit corners and defeats the Goblin.
- **Arc 3 — The Void Encounter:** Chance ventures into the inverted-color depths and meets the **Black Hero** — *a guardian of the empty spaces.* Chance barely escapes. Spirit, far away, senses the Five Friends fighting Makuta and sends them healing energy.
- **Arc 4 — The Race Finishes:** They arrive simultaneously at the floating island where the Mask hums, *not wanting to be found*, wanting to test the worthy. *"It is just a mask!" — "It is the balance of the world!"*
- **Arc 5 — The Final Wager:** The player must choose. *Channel Aid* to save the Friends, or *Focus on the Mask* and ignore them. Then: *I am Spirit (finish ritual)* or *I am Chance (take Mask).* The loser becomes the final boss; the winner becomes canon.

**Theme:** *Order vs. Chaos. Discipline vs. freedom. Duty vs. opportunity. There is no middle ground.*

### Waker of Winds — *The Awakening* (Air Hero)

The Air Hero wakes on the floating stones of the **Sky Palace**, where the wind whispers: *"Prove your worth."* The campaign is a graduated trial — Speed, Flow, Precision, No-Damage challenges — climbing toward the throne. Along the way the Air Hero faces a Cloud Golem, a Storm Crow, a Tornado Machina, and a Doppelgänger of themselves (their own doubt). The Fire Hero arrives as an ally for the final ascent. At the top: *"The King was once a hero like you. He tests you now."* Victory: Air becomes the new Storm King.

**Theme:** *Worthiness through trial. Mastery through repetition. Accepting one's place in an ancient lineage.*

### Symphony of Sickness — *The Great Infection* (Poison Hero)

A villain campaign told from inside corruption. The Poison Hero wakes in the swamp with black veins and a smile. *"You are not sick — you are sickness."* The arc is the methodical conquest of every elemental biome through **infection**, not destruction:

1. **The Swamp** — home, throne, first vector.
2. **The Living Forest** — corrupted at critical contagion mass; the canopy doesn't die, it *transforms*.
3. **The Poisoned Spring** — water rivers turn iridescent green-black, carrying the chemistry downstream.
4. **The Last Resistance (Fire)** — fire is nature's sanitizer, but the survivors of fire are the most dangerous things alive. Fire turns sickly green.
5. **The Iron Lung (Metal)** — machines seize, joints corrode, sensors blind. *Not in fire, but in rust and slime.*
6. **The Frozen Vault (Ice)** — cold doesn't kill it, cold *preserves* it.

By Wave 41 the world is fully infected and self-sustaining: *"You are no longer spreading the infection. You are the infection. Personified. Anthropomorphic. Inevitable."* The final boss is the Poison Hero's own shadow — the immune response of a world that fought back and lost. *"You are the cure or the final disease. There is no difference anymore."*

**Theme:** *Corruption as inevitable transformation. The world as ecosystem. Becoming the system itself.*

### Symphony of Sickness — *The Conductor's Awakening* (Sound Hero)

A parallel campaign in the same DLC, but the conquest is gentler in form and just as totalizing in scope. The Sound Hero stands on the **Resonant Plain** and decides to make the world an orchestra. Each elemental biome is reframed as a stage: the **Ember Stage** (Fire), the **Deep Amphitheater** (Water), the **Jungle Chorus** (Plant), the **Crystal Hall** (Ice), the **Machine Amphitheater** (Metal). In each, three **Resonance Totems** must be claimed; once tuned, the biome learns to vibrate in the conductor's key. *"The fire doesn't have to stop burning. It just has to burn in time."*

By Wave 35 all five converted biomes vibrate at once — *a chord that spans the world.* But behind the conductor walks a shadow that has been listening to every note and composing its inverse. The campaign ends at the **Great Bell** at the center of the world: *"You cannot harmonize the world without first harmonizing yourself."* The final boss is the **Silent Shadow** — the accumulated quiet of a world that once had no song.

**Theme:** *Harmony as conquest. Rhythm as control. Silence as the shadow of music.*

### Echos of Eternity — Time, Love, and the Maze
A trio of dream-and-time-themed mechanics: the Time Hero, the Love Hero, and the **Maze of Time** as a structural conceit. Time loops, love across moments, eternity as a biome. (The campaign script is more sparse than the others; mechanics carry most of the meaning here.)

### Disciples of Deception — Psycho, Mirror, Smoke
A character pack. No story campaign, but each hero is a thematic refraction of deception: Psycho (chaos disruptor with a Hysteria meter), Mirror (defensive reflector), and Smoke (area denial). Their biomes — the **Hall of Mirrors**, the **Mindscape**, and the **Smog Quarter** — are each a metaphor for a kind of lie.

### Radiance of Ruin — Light, Thorn, Dream
A character pack. No story campaign, but each hero turns a wound into a resource. Light's *Mask Integrity* is a fragile shell that grants escalating power as it cracks. Thorn's *Blood Bond* trades HP for damage. Dream's *Lucidity* gauge lets her phase through reality itself. Together they are the trinity of **sacrifice, pain, and doubt as resources.**

## 8. Overarching Throughlines

1. **The Five Friends' inner journey** — from isolated individuals with raw powers to an integrated team whose flaws complete each other.
2. **Makuta's external threat** — not domination but *exposure*: forcing the world to confront what it has buried.
3. **The Green Goblin's private vendetta** — a grieving genius using everyone (including Makuta) as an instrument of personal control.
4. **The DLCs as thematic variations on the core question of balance:**
   - *Champions of Chaos* — unity through difference
   - *Faith of Fortune* — order vs. chaos as eternal duality
   - *Waker of Winds* — worthiness and lineage
   - *Symphony of Sickness (Poison)* — transformation as conquest
   - *Symphony of Sickness (Sound)* — harmony as conquest
   - *Radiance of Ruin* — wound as resource
5. **The Museum as meta-narrative** — a sanctuary of reflection where heroes and enemies are preserved and studied. The player is, ultimately, collecting *the truth of what each being is.*

## 9. The Mask, Reread

Five readings of the Golden Mask, all canonical, all in tension:

- **The Goblin** sees a tool: *"tools belong to whoever understands them best."*
- **Spirit** sees a burden: *"The ritual must be completed."*
- **Chance** sees a prize: *"Jackpot!"*
- **Makuta** sees a lie: *"It silenced fear instead of addressing it."*
- **The Mask itself**, when approached, hums and *does not want to be found.* It wants to test the worthy.

That last reading may be the only one that matters.
